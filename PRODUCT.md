# Product

## Register

brand

The storefront is the primary surface and is brand-led: the design _is_ the
product experience. The admin dashboard (`/admin/*`) is a secondary, product-register
surface; treat it with product thinking (clarity, density, task speed) while
keeping the same brand foundations (type, color, voice).

## Users

**Gift-givers**, not bargain-hunters. Someone marking a moment: a birthday, an
anniversary, a wedding, a new baby, a corporate thank-you. They arrive with a
person in mind and a feeling they want to deliver, often a little time-pressured
and wanting reassurance that the gift will look and feel considered when it
lands. Many are buying from or for Sri Lanka (pricing is in LKR; the brand is
based in Kuliyapitiya). They care about presentation, trust (will it arrive, will
it look premium), and ease of choosing. The job to be done: _find and send a gift
that feels personal and beautifully made, without second-guessing it._

Secondary user: the **shop owner / admin**, managing products, gift packages,
categories, and orders. Their job is fast, confident catalog and order
management, not browsing.

## Product Purpose

THE MOON CHARM is a curated gift house: individual products and hand-assembled
gift packages for occasions, sold online with cart, checkout, accounts, and order
tracking. It exists to make giving feel intentional, the opposite of a generic
marketplace. Success looks like a visitor trusting the brand enough to buy a gift
sight-unseen because the site itself signals care and quality.

## Brand Personality

Warm, romantic, and crafted. Three words: **tender, considered, timeless.**

Voice: speaks like a thoughtful gift-giver, not a marketer. Plain, warm,
specific. Talks about the moment and the person, never about "deals" or
"solutions." Confident enough to be quiet. Editorial luxury with a heartbeat:
the warmth of a hand-written note, the restraint of a fine boutique. Hermes is
the nearest touchstone (warm color, white space, craft, rich imagery, a little
wit), reinterpreted softer and more romantic for gifting.

Emotional goal: a visitor should feel _cared for_ and slightly moved, the way
you feel choosing a gift for someone you love.

## Anti-references

- **Generic Shopify / dropshipping template.** No stock hero-with-two-columns,
  no badge soup, no off-the-shelf storefront grid that could belong to any store.
- **Loud, discount-driven commerce.** No big red SALE, countdown timers, "ONLY 2
  LEFT" urgency, or aggressive deal-pushing. Value is shown calmly.
- **The previous identity.** The glassmorphic violet to fuchsia to rose gradient
  system, gradient text, and frosted-glass cards are retired completely. Do not
  reintroduce gradient text or decorative blur.
- **Cold, corporate, or techy.** No sterile SaaS-dashboard feel on the storefront.
  It should read as human and made-by-hand, never as a B2B tool.
- **AI-editorial cliche.** Avoid the saturated "display-serif-italic + tiny mono
  eyebrows + three ruled columns + no imagery" magazine affectation. Editorial
  here means generous space and real photography, not a Fraunces-and-rules costume.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the gift be the hero.** Photography and the product carry the emotion.
   The interface frames them and gets out of the way; it never competes with a
   colored panel where an image belongs.
2. **Warmth from the brand, not the canvas.** The honey-amber primary, the
   romantic claret accent, the typography, and the imagery carry the warmth. The
   surface stays clean and bright so the warmth reads as intentional, not muddy.
3. **Quiet confidence over urgency.** Persuade through craft and clarity, not
   pressure. Calm pacing, honest copy, no manufactured scarcity.
4. **Considered, not decorated.** Every flourish earns its place. Restraint is
   the luxury signal; ornament for its own sake reads cheap.
5. **Make giving effortless.** The path from "I need a gift" to "it's on its way"
   should feel guided and reassuring at every step, especially cart and checkout.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text >=4.5:1 against its background; large/bold text

> =3:1; placeholder text held to the same 4.5:1. White text on the saturated
> honey/claret fills must clear AA for the text size used. Full keyboard operability
> with visible focus states, semantic landmarks, and labelled controls. Honor
> `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade or instant alternative for every
> animation. Currency, dates, and copy default to a Sri Lankan English context.
