The Moon Charm — finish the two moments shoppers wait on

Overall

This is a genuinely well-built motion base — a production-grade reveal system, a restrained image zoom, real reduced-motion handling. What's missing is finish in two directions: the product gallery and the cart badge, the two moments a shopper waits on, have no motion at all, while the hero spends 800ms fading in text that should arrive instantly. Differentiate the tempo, animate the two snapping moments, and it reads as expensive.

02

Where the timings land

Duration is a budget. A slow 700ms zoom on a product photo is correct — a hover that wants to feel languid earns the time. The mismatch here is the opposite of the usual one: the heaviest motion is decorative, and the two functional moments have none. Each dot is one of the redesign's animations, plotted where it runs today (scale extends to 800ms to fit the hero).

Instant / Responsive · 0–300ms — where most interaction belongs. Taps, hovers, nav, state changes.
Deliberate · 300–500ms — earned by larger surfaces and brand flourishes: sheets, dialogs.
Sluggish · 500ms+ — laggy for UI, but legitimate for a slow luxury image zoom or scroll reveal.
What's off

The slowest things in the system are the ones the user never acts on — the hero (1) fades text in over 800ms, reveals (3) run 700ms. Meanwhile swapping a product photo (7) and watching the cart count change (8) — the two moments a shopper actually waits on — have no transition at all. Motion is heaviest where it's decorative and absent where it's functional.

Issues to address
ImportantJakub

The product gallery hard-swaps its main image with no crossfade

WhatOn the product page, clicking a thumbnail or a prev/next arrow replaces the main image's src instantly. The activeImage is a single <Image> keyed off state, so the old photo cuts to the new one with no transition.

Why it mattersThis is the showcase moment on a luxury PDP — the one place a shopper is studying the product. A hard cut reads as a re-render, not a gallery. Everywhere else the redesign is gentle; here it snaps, and the contrast is felt.

Recommended motionCrossfade the active image: a 250–280ms opacity fade with a tiny scale(1.03 → 1) settle, on your own --ease-out. Key a wrapper on activeIndex so React remounts and replays it, or cross-dissolve two stacked layers. Subtle, but it turns a swap into a transition.

The cart count badge appears and increments with no transition

WhatIn the navbar, the count badge renders conditionally (count > 0) and the number is replaced in place. Add an item and the badge just pops into existence; add another and the digit hard-swaps.

Why it mattersAdd-to-cart is the payoff of the whole store, and the badge is its only confirmation. A silent appearance leaves the most rewarding micro-moment feeling like nothing happened — the user isn't sure the click registered.

Recommended motionPop the badge in with scale(0.6 → 1) + opacity over ~220ms ease-out (no bounce — this fires often). For the increment, a quick opacity+translateY roll on the digit. It confirms the action without becoming a toy.

Secondary lens

Mixed

What's working well
  • ✓The single easing token is a custom curve — cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1), not the default ease. This is exactly Emil's point: easing is the most important part, and a strong ease-out beats the built-ins everywhere. app/globals.css:85

  • ✓Nothing animates on keyboard-driven navigation, and high-frequency actions stay quiet. Keyboard users aren't taxed with motion they didn't ask for.
Issues to address
ImportantEmil

The hero fades its heading in from invisible over 800ms

WhatThe hero applies mc-animate-rise (opacity 0 → 1 + 20px rise, 800ms) to the eyebrow, the h1, the paragraph, the CTA row, and the caption, staggered 90ms apart. The headline — the page's likely Largest Contentful Paint element — starts fully transparent and finishes arriving close to a second after paint.

Why it mattersThis is motion-on-mount for static text, and it's the slowest thing in the system. Animating the LCP heading from opacity: 0 delays perceived load — the first thing a visitor came to read is the last thing to appear. Emil's rule: motion should serve a purpose, not announce the entrance of body copy.

Recommended motionKeep a hero entrance, but make it tight: opacity + ~12px rise over ~320ms, and limit it to the headline plus one supporting element rather than all five. Leave the eyebrow and caption static. The moment still feels composed without holding the reader behind it.

Which lens carried this audit

Referenced most

Jakub Krehel — Production Polish

The Moon Charm's gaps are craft gaps, not taste gaps: a strong system with two unfinished state changes and a single, undifferentiated tempo. That's Jakub's territory — finishing what's been started and giving motion context-appropriate timing — so his lens drove the ordering. Emil set where the durations belong; Jhey marked the one place left to spend delight.

  • Lean JakubAudit every state change for a missing transition. Give the gallery and cart their crossfades, and introduce a small tempo scale instead of one curve for everything.
  • Lean EmilTreat duration as a budget tied to frequency and surface. Pull the hero off 800ms, keep the slow timings only on the deliberate image zoom.
  • Lean JheyPick the single highest-emotion element — the moon mark — and over-invest in one moment there, with a one-shot draw-on or a success tick.
